Purple Teaming in Cyber Academia

"Purple Teaming" is a concept in cybersecurity education where participants both attack (red) and defend (blue) their designated networks against other teams. These fast-paced engagements reinforce essential cybersecurity skills and require participants to think creatively and act quickly. More importantly, defending networks as a team encourages cooperative play and teamwork, as a single individual cannot manage everything at once. This fosters leadership skills and collaboration, which are often lacking in many cybersecurity activities.

However, implementing this concept in a classroom setting can be challenging, as commercial cyber ranges are often expensive, and maintaining on-premises servesr can be difficult without a dedicated team. One effective solution is to use 'GNS-3' (Graphical Network Simulator 3), a free and open-source tool that allows the incorporation of attack and defense scenarios. With GNS-3, students can create their own simulated environments without the high costs of real equipment, enabling them to connect their networks with those of other students to test their defenses and identify vulnerabilities.

While many cybersecurity students and professionals build their own exploitable environments for learning and testing, this approach often fails to develop some of the most critical skills of a penetration tester: reconnaissance and enumeration. How can one learn to perform network discovery legally when they already know their own network? By establishing a system where students can connect their simualted networks, they can practice these often-overlooked skills of an ethical hacker against oen another in a safe and repeatable manner.
